Monday June 15 – Thursday June 18 · 3 nights
Monday · 15 June
Arrive Ubud — Culture Night
1:00 PM
Check In — Jungle Villa Pool
Ubud feels entirely different from Seminyak: cooler (~24°C), quieter, lush green. Let the kids settle. The sound of jungle birds and water is immediate.
4:00 PM
Walk Jl. Hanoman & Ubud Town
Flat and stroller-friendly. Buy sarongs here for temple visits (IDR 50–80k each — you'll need them for Tirta Empul and Goa Gajah tomorrow). Browse silver, batik, and local art.
6:30 PM
Ubud Palace — Kecak or Legong Dance
Held nightly at the Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) in the heart of Ubud. Sara will be completely transfixed by the costumes, gamelan music, and fire. Shlok may nap through it — still absolutely worth going. ~IDR 100k per adult. Arrive 20 min early for good seats.
Tuesday · 16 June
Rice Terraces, Coconuts & Monkeys
7:30 AM
Tegallalang Rice Terraces
Go early — extraordinary morning light, thin crowds before 9 AM. The green terraces are spectacular in June. Bring a carrier/sling for Shlok on the uneven paths. Sara will love running between the paddies.
9:00 AM
Mupu Rice Terrace — Hidden Gem (★4.9)
Just next to Tegallalang. A family-owned plot with a warm, chatty owner who explains the rice growing cycle and sells huge fresh coconuts. Low-key, authentic, no tourist crowds. A highlight of the whole trip.
10:00 AM
Coffee Stop — Terrace Café with Pool
Several cafés along Tegallalang have shallow infinity pools overlooking the paddies. Kids can splash in the shallow end while adults have their first proper coffee of the morning.
12:00 PM
Villa Lunch + Toddler Nap 🧒 essential nap
Don't push through this. Shlok and Sara will be exhausted from an active morning outdoors. The afternoon activity (Monkey Forest) needs them fresh and happy.
3:00 PM
Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary
Sara will absolutely love this — she'll talk about "the monkeys" for months. Key rules: keep Shlok in arms/carrier at all times (monkeys grab), no eye contact with monkeys, no food/plastic bags/shiny items. Rangers are helpful and approachable. ~IDR 120k per adult. Allow 2 hours. Rating: ★4.5, 58,000+ reviews.
7:00 PM
Dinner — Locavore or Hujan Locale
Locavore is one of SE Asia's finest restaurants. The adults can rotate: half go for early seating, half stay with the toddlers at the villa, then swap for drinks/dessert nearby. Book Locavore 2–3 weeks ahead minimum.
Wednesday · 17 June
Temples, Water & A Slow Afternoon
8:30 AM
Tirta Empul — Sacred Water Temple
One of Bali's most sacred sites: a spring-fed purification pool where Balinese Hindus conduct ritual bathing. You can participate or respectfully observe. Sarong required (provided at gate). Sara is perfectly old enough to understand "holy water" — she will remember this.
10:30 AM
Goa Gajah — Elephant Cave Temple
9th-century rock-carved temple with a famous demon-face entrance. Kids are fascinated by the carved stone figure. A 20 min drive from Tirta Empul. Quick stop, ~45 min total.
1:00 PM
Villa Return — Floating Breakfast Setup + Afternoon Pool
Book the floating breakfast presentation the evening before with the villa — flowers, fruit platters, smoothies arranged in the pool. Beautiful for photos, beautiful for the kids.
4:00 PM
Group Spa — Karsa Spa or In-Villa
Karsa Spa has a children's play area so adults can actually relax. Book 6+ slots simultaneously. Traditional Balinese massage ~IDR 250k per 90 min. Pre-book 1–2 weeks ahead for a group.
5:30 PM
Campuhan Ridge Walk — Golden Hour
Free 2km walk along a jungle ridge overlooking two rivers and rice fields. Best at golden hour. Take the carrier for Shlok. Sara can walk most of it. One of Ubud's most peaceful experiences.
7:30 PM
Farewell Dinner — In-Villa Balinese Feast
Last night all together. Have the villa chef prepare a full traditional spread: babi guling (suckling pig), bebek betutu (slow-roasted duck), lawar, satay lilit, and pisang goreng. Everyone around one table. Pack bags after dinner.

Tips & Essentials
👶
Travelling with Shlok (16 months)
Bring a soft structured carrier (ERGObaby, Lillebaby) — temples have uneven stone steps, rice terraces have muddy narrow paths. Strollers won't work everywhere. Keep him in the carrier at Monkey Forest — monkeys are curious and will grab bags/items. Request cot from both villas. Pack familiar snacks. Night flights: he may not sleep — prepare tablet and sensory toys.
🧒
Travelling with Sara (3 years)
Sara is at a perfect age for Bali. She'll remember the monkeys, fire dancers, temple carvings, and pool forever. Buy her a small sarong at Jl. Hanoman — kids love wearing them at temples. The Ubud Palace dance is magical for this age. Keep a change of clothes accessible in the day bag — she will find both water and mud.
🏊
Pool Safety — Non-Negotiable
Always request a pool fence or safety net before arrival at both villas. With Shlok (16 months) this is non-negotiable. Most quality villas provide this at no charge on request. State it in your booking message, your confirmation email, and once more by WhatsApp 2 days before arrival. Don't assume it will be there.
🌡️
June Weather — Ideal Timing
Early dry season — the best time of year to visit Bali. Days 28–31°C, low humidity, almost no rain, cool Ubud evenings (~22°C). Apply SPF 50+ every 90 min on the kids — tropical sun is deceptive even when cloudy. Ubud mornings at the rice terraces can be misty and cool — bring a light layer.
🚐
Group Transport
Book a dedicated 12-seater minibus for all major transfers: DPS airport arrival (Jun 13), Seminyak→Ubud (Jun 15), and Ubud→DPS (Jun 18). ~IDR 600–900k per trip, split 8 ways = almost nothing. Have the villa arrange it — they know trusted drivers. Never rely on multiple Grab rides with 2 toddlers and a mountain of luggage.
🛕
Temple Etiquette
All visitors must wear a sarong below the knee (provided or sold at gates, ~IDR 30–50k). Speak quietly inside. Never step over temple offerings on the ground — step around them. Avoid pointing with one finger; use the whole hand. Children can enter all the temples on this itinerary. A genuinely respectful attitude will earn smiles from local worshippers.
🇦🇺
Sitasma & Ajnish (Australia)
Direct flights to Bali from Sydney ~6h, Melbourne ~6.5h, Brisbane ~7h. Confirm DPS arrival time immediately so you can coordinate the airport minibus. Australian citizens need a Bali Visa on Arrival (VOA) — USD 35 per person at DPS airport. Have USD cash ready or use the airport VOA card payment machine.
💊
Health & Medicine
Pack one shared kit: DEET mosquito repellent (especially for evenings), oral rehydration salts for the kids, antihistamine, paracetamol for toddlers, sunburn cream, baby formula/food for Shlok if required. Avoid ice from street stalls, only bottled water. Good pharmacies (Apotek) are widely available. Bali belly is possible — go easy on the first day.
💸
Money & Cash
Cash is king in Bali. Withdraw IDR at DPS airport ATMs on arrival — competitive rates and always available. Avoid street money changers (scam risk). Use official changers at Seminyak Square or your villa. Approximate: IDR 100,000 ≈ AUD 10 / USD 6. Good restaurant meal: IDR 100–200k per person. Bali massage: IDR 150–250k per hour.
📱
Connectivity & Coordination
Buy a Telkomsel SIM at the DPS airport (best coverage island-wide, ~IDR 100k for 10–15GB). Create a WhatsApp group for all 8 adults before you land. Share villa addresses and the driver's WhatsApp in the group. Download Bali maps offline on Google Maps before you fly — some areas have patchy signal.
🙏
Cultural Respect
Bali is predominantly Hindu. Step around offerings (small flower baskets on the ground) — never over them. A simple "Selamat pagi" (good morning) or "Terima kasih" (thank you) earns genuine warmth everywhere. Tipping is not mandatory but deeply appreciated — IDR 20–50k for good service feels right.
🎒
Packing Smart for 10
Pack one shared first-aid kit rather than duplicating across bags. Reef-safe sunscreen (better for ocean swims). A portable bluetooth speaker for villa evenings. Light linen shirts/dresses for restaurants — it gets warm quickly. Laundry at the villa is ~IDR 15,000/kg. Pack light — you'll buy things you didn't know you needed in Seminyak.
